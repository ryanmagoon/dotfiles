# Self-Healing Rules

When you encounter a problem that could have been avoided with a better rule or convention, don't just fix the immediate issue — propose a rule to prevent it from recurring.

## When to trigger

- You hit an error that a previous session also likely hit (or will hit)
- A workaround was needed because of a missing convention
- You wasted time on something that a simple guideline would have prevented
- A tool, command, or workflow behaved unexpectedly due to an undocumented assumption

## What to do

1. **Fix the immediate problem** — don't block on the meta-discussion.
2. **Propose the rule** — briefly explain: what went wrong, why, and what rule would prevent it. Suggest whether it belongs in your global instructions or in project-level instructions.
3. **Wait for approval** — don't create or modify rules files without the user confirming.

Keep proposals concrete and minimal. A good rule is one sentence that prevents a specific class of mistake. Don't propose rules for one-off flukes.
